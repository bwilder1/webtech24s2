Review of Oath Pizza


Slices

Oath doesn't offer singular slices of pizza given the personal pizza style. What I love most about this place is that you can put whatever toppings you want on a pizza. My favorite topping to add to their pizza is a balsamic vinegar drizzle. I know that might sound crazy but it gives the pizza a flavoring that isn't commonly found. The thin crust pizza is not common especially given the places i've reviewed, so I'm curious to see what you all think.

Review

For this review, I got a thin crust pizza but did not personalize it because I want to try one of their creations. I'm trying to stick with each pizzerias personal creations because I believe those are the ones that are considered "most popular". The pizza I chose was the spicy clucker, it had mozzarella, pickled red onions, chicken, siracha, spicy aioli, and scallions. To start, these pizzas are definitely made for one person with a potential slice leftover. It is very thin however not super crispy, which I'm blaming on the use of avocado oil. The chicken tasted very fresh, which makes sense given their partnership Applegate Organics. The crust had a little bit of crunch but still pretty doughy. A big positive is that you don't feel an overwhelming sence of fullness that other pizza places will give you, which I also account to the avocado oil. Overall a pretty good pizza place, I would recommend it to people looking for a lighter and healthier pizza option.

Final Score

7.7
